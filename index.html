Project Overview

This project focuses on the development and optimization of a Direct Methanol Fuel Cell (DMFC) system. Our goal is to design a high-efficiency DMFC that can reliably generate electrical energy from methanol while minimizing methanol crossover and improving fuel utilization.

We are testing different operating conditions, including fuel concentration, feed rates, and geometric configurations. Additionally, we aim to fabricate our own Membrane Electrode Assembly (MEA) to explore cost-effective alternatives.

Project Progress

We have successfully completed the initial setup of the fuel cell stack and achieved stable voltage output under controlled conditions. The control system (using Arduino) is partially integrated, and early tests have shown promising results.

We are currently working on optimizing fuel delivery using peristaltic pumps and exploring the impact of different methanol concentrations on performance.

Experimental Results

Our testing indicates that the fuel cell outputs approximately 19W under 50 mA/cm² operating conditions, increasing to ~30W at 100 mA/cm². Methanol crossover remains within acceptable limits at 1M concentration.
