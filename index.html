Project Overview

This project explores the design and prototyping of a Direct Methanol Fuel Cell (DMFC) system using a 10-cell Flex-Stak from the Fuel Cell Store. The cell operates on 3% methanol solution (~1M), and is designed to produce sufficient voltage and current to charge a 20,000 mAh battery pack within 24 hours.

Our prototype integrates fluid dynamics (via peristaltic pumping), real-time monitoring (via Arduino and sensors), and electrical circuitry for power conditioning. We aim to validate theoretical predictions of fuel consumption, current density (~50â€“100 mA/cmÂ²), and overall efficiency (~20â€“40%).

Key design constraints include minimizing methanol crossover, maintaining membrane hydration, and safely handling flammable liquids.

Introduction

Our project investigates the design, construction, and optimization of a Direct Methanol Fuel Cell (DMFC) system. DMFCs convert the chemical energy of methanol into electrical energy via redox reactions, offering a compact and clean energy source for portable applications.

How to Build and Run the Setup

Materials Used

  • 10-cell Direct Methanol Flex-Stak
  • Peristaltic pump (5V), silicone tubing
  • Arduino Uno, voltage & current sensors
  • 1M methanol solution (prepared at 3% by weight)
  • Custom circuit with MOSFET & load resistor

Setup Steps

  1. Assemble the DMFC stack and torque screws evenly (8â€“10 in-lbs).
  2. Hydrate membrane with distilled water for 15 minutes.
  3. Prepare and begin pumping 3% methanol solution at 100 cc/min.
  4. Connect electrodes to Arduino-controlled data logging circuit.
  5. Monitor voltage output over time and adjust flow rate as needed.
